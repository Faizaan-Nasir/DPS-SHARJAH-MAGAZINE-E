How to Choose your Passion?





It’s just impossible to talk about what you want to be before you are in 9th. Because let’s be honest, we all are immature and we arbitrarily change our minds about what we want to be. Like for example I have changed my mind 6 times before ninth.

  -> First I wanted be a pilot,

  -> Then I changed my mind in kindergarten 2, and wanted to be like my father, a Civil Engineer.

  -> Then I decided I wanted to be a software engineer like my brother since he got soo many privileges.

  -> Then I also considered becoming a politician. Change the world and make it a better place. You know the drill.

  -> Then I decided I will be better off being an aeronautical engineer. That would fullfill my dream of becoming a pilot as well as an engineer, or that’s what I thought.

  -> Then I found that becoming a pilot is still the best option.

 After reading that, you may now doubt my ability to give you tips on this topic. But since I have the authority to do so, I’ll do it anyway. First of all no one wants you to be the perfect human in life so you can be inconsistent like I am. Change your mind if you see a better option. No one’s gonna judge you. And even if Sharma ji judges you, why should it matter to you? It's your life, not his. That being said you can’t be extremely inconsistent either. In my personal opinion, by the time you reach grade 10, you need to be sure of what field you select. Next thing is don’t select a field that you know might not suit you in the long run, like say a YouTuber, every teenagers dream job.

See the problem is the YouTube field is also very unpredictable, it changes every now and then. Similar to my previous point, don’t lock onto one particular field and never move on. Let me be clear here, many-a-times we don’t consider the economical situation and the manpower really needed for the field we have chosen. So if you know you wont get a job in the field you have chosen, then chose some other one. Well that’s my tips on how to select your job and consider READING it for once. Maybe by the end of your read you’ll be able to relate with me and hopefully will find what you want to be.
